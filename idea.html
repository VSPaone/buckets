UNFILL / REFILL

The Living Bucket Deck

A physical game for Buckets/. Every card comes from real buckets on real streets. You don’t brainstorm problems. You face them - and decide what to keep, what to unfill, and what to move.

The only rule: no card is allowed to sit in silence.
How it’s born
Street Buckets & Traveling Cart
  • Labeled buckets in public spaces invite handwritten notes: frictions, duties, leaks, quiet truths.
  • A traveling cart repeats this in places that never get a microphone.
  • Every slip stays human: no jargon washed over it.
The Deck
  • Collected notes are distilled into cards - each card = one real pattern or tension.
  • No fiction. No “imagined user”. Only what people actually poured in.
How you play
The loop
1 Draw a card.
2 Name who carries it now.
3 Place it: KEEP / UNFILL / SHIFT.
4 Write one clear sentence.
5 Move on. No parking.
  • Play with 4–8 people: teams, communities, departments, GovJam groups.
  • The power is not in the props - it’s in making every hidden weight visible and decided.
Three decision buckets
Keep We accept this weight. It matters. We state why and how we’ll own it.
Unfill We stop, simplify, or release this. Emptying creates room for better work.
Shift This moves from the wrong hands to the right ones - closer to those affected.
Rituals
Unfill Wall UNFILL cards are displayed then destroyed or archived in public - a visible choice to stop.
Shift Map SHIFT cards are redrawn as arrows from overloaded roles to new stewards.
Keep With Intent KEEP cards stay pinned with reasons, so “burden” becomes conscious duty.
The Chronicle Sessions feed a simple zine/poster: what this community chose to carry, drop, or move.
Why it fits Buckets/
  • Buckets expose limits: you see what’s full, empty, or leaking.
  • “/” is the shift: from adding more to deciding differently.
  • UNFILL / REFILL turns that into a repeatable, low-tech civic game.
  • You can run it with three boxes and printed cards. No app required.
The game is a frame. The content is whatever your people drop into the buckets.
